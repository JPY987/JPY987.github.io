Your Rights

What to do if ICE stops you in public:

  • Stay Calm - Don't run, argue, or fight the agent.
  • Ask, "Am I free to go?" - If the answer is yes, you can walk away. If not, invoke your right to remain silent and ask to speak with a lawyer
  • You have the right to remain silent - You do not have to answer any questions, including where you were born or how you entered the U.S.
  • ICE can only search you with consent - If an agent tries to search your belongings, say "I do not consent to this search."

What to do if ICE comes to your house:

  • Do NOT open the door - ICE cannot enter your house without a valid judicial warrant.
  • Ask ICE to see the warrant - Ask the agent to slide the warrant under the door or show it through a window to verify its validity.
  • You have the right to remain silent - You do not have to answer ICE's questions.
  • Do NOT sign anything - Speak with a lawyer before signing anything. Signing documents may waive your rights or lead to deportation.
  • ICE may lie to get you to open the door - Agents often pretend to be local police conducting an investigation.

What to do after an ICE arrest:

  • Do NOT answer questions - You have the right to remain silent.
  • Do NOT sign anything - Many ICE documents are designed to speed up deportation. Always consult an attorney first.
  • Ask to speak to a lawyer - The government will NOT provide you with a free lawyer, but you have the right to hire one.
